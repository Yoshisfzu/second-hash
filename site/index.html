How Boost Modules Work

A Boost Module is a small add-on component crafted from retired miner parts. It attaches directly to an existing miner in your facility grid, improving that miner's effective hashrate or power efficiency without taking up a separate slot.

Modules remain active as long as they stay attached. You can remove a boost at any time — but removal permanently destroys the module. The components are consumed during detachment and cannot be recovered.

This mechanic creates a meaningful strategic decision: boost now and commit, or hold the module for a better deployment later.

Non-Reversible on Removal Detaching a Boost Module from a miner permanently burns the module NFT. This is by design — it creates sustained deflationary pressure on the network's NFT supply while rewarding committed operators.

How It Works

The Second Hash Trade-In Program takes miners you are no longer using — USB sticks, first-gen GPUs, old rigs that draw more power than they are worth — and converts them into refabricated SH-series machines.

Your old miners are permanently decommissioned during the process. The components are harvested, tested, and reassembled into a new unit with optimized power delivery and tuned firmware. Nothing is wasted.

The result: your dead inventory turns into live hashrate, and the network gets cleaner.

01

Select Your Trade-In

Choose which retired miners to submit. Each SH model requires specific component types and quantities as source material.

02

Pay Assembly Fee

Cover the refabrication cost in hCASH. This fee funds component testing, power rerouting, firmware tuning, and final QA.

03

Components Are Decommissioned

Your submitted miners are permanently burned. This is non-reversible — the original NFTs are removed from circulation.

04

Receive Your SH Miner

A new Second Hash miner NFT is minted to your wallet, ready to be placed in your facility grid and start hashing.

About
The Second Hash Story

We did not set out to build mining hardware. We set out to solve a problem.

As the HashCash network matured through successive halvings, we watched a pattern repeat: each wave of new, more efficient hardware rendered the previous generation economically dead. USB miners that once earned meaningful rewards became wallet clutter. First-gen GPUs that cost real AVAX to acquire sat idle, consuming power slots that could be better used.

The standard answer was "just buy new hardware." But not everyone can. And even for those who can, the old machines still exist — sitting in wallets, listed on the marketplace for prices nobody will pay, taking up space in a network that would be healthier without them.

Second Hash exists because we believe hardware does not have to die when the math changes. The silicon still works. The compute paths are still functional. What failed is the configuration — the power delivery, the firmware tuning, the architecture-level decisions that were optimal at launch but are not optimal now.

So we strip them down, test every component, and rebuild them into machines tuned for today's network conditions. The result is not as efficient as the latest Jvidia or Lightning flagship. It was never meant to be. It is meant to be the best possible machine you can build from parts that would otherwise contribute nothing.

We are not competing with the manufacturers. We are cleaning up after them — and giving every miner in the HashCash ecosystem a second chance to hash.

— The Second Hash Workshop
